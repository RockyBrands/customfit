The Cost of Occupational Fatigue

See You at SHRM23 Booth 3859

Statistics

• 13% of injuries in the workplace can be attributed to fatigue

• 16% of workers experience at least 1 near miss or safety incident due to fatigue

• 27% of workers have fallen asleep unintentionally on the job

NSC report, Fatigue In The Workplace: Causes & Consequences of Employee Fatigue

Fatigue costs US employers more than $136 Billion a year

• Absenteeism - Days off for recovery, doctors appts, physical therapy

• Presenteeism - Reduced work performance accounts for 84% of $136 billion

Fatigued workers lose an average 2.3 hours of productivity a week1

• 110 hours a year = $1,650 to $3,300 annually per employee

• This equates to between $1.6 - $3.3 million a year2

I. Source: Journal of Occupational and Environmental Medicine vol 49, No1, Jan. 2007
II. 1 - More than non-fatigued workers
III. 2 - Based on 40 hour days, 48 work weeks a year at $15 - $30 an hour for 1,000 employees

Fight Fatigue with CustomFit Wellness

Manage the risk and reduce the cost with an out-of-box CustomFit Employee Wellness Benefit that provides your employees greater energy and less likelihood of injury. Improve work culture and retention while offering a competitive benefit for new hires.

Orthotics
  • Correct and ensure proper body alignment to prevent unnecessary muscle exertion and ligament stress

  • Full Foot Contact Area equals more evenly distributed body weight pressure to reduce overworked muscles

  • Cushioned comfort and shock-absorbing materials help reduce impact stress
Insoles
  • Pressure-responsive materials provide cushioned support, smooth energy transfer and return

  • Heel cup activates body's natural shock absorber by pulling soft tissue together to add cushion to the bone and prevent stress

  • Fills the empty space in your shoes to disperse pressure impact
Compression Socks
  • Improves blood circulation to the heart which increases levels of oxygen throughout the body that refreshes and energizes muscles

  • Oxygen level increase can help prevent cognitive delay and distraction

  • Reduces swelling and edema

  • Reduces build-up of lactic acid which can cause “heavy legs”
